Contemporary Portraiture (3 Artists)

I don’t really know anything about trends in art or what’s going on with contemporary art right now. I’ll start with that. But my understanding is that contemporary portraiture is all about context and all about flattening things down. Subjects are de-contextualized and re-contextualized, they or their surroundings are flattened down and distorted with flat colors and stretched out shapes. The subject is central, but they are central within a context, which cannot be forgotten.

I’ll start off with a big name in contemporary portraiture, Kehinde Wiley, whose work is incredible, vibrant, and energetic. I’m really drawn to his backgrounds more than anything, he traps his subjects against busy busy backgrounds, leaves them floating, surrounds them with leaves and flowers and patterns. But they feel free and unconstrained. He paints his subjects in poses from famous historical paintings. Contemporary subjects posed as figures of yore floating in patterned space. Of course, we start with his famous portrait of Barack Obama:

It is harder to find high quality photos of his work than I would have expected.

Next up is Jordan Casteel, whose work is about every day people and their shared experiences and struggles. Subjects are painted in places they’re familiar with: their homes, the subway, their stoops. And she reinforces my belief that really, heads and hands are what it’s all about.

I can’t stop looking at this painting above. What a beautiful work.

And last for today, Emilio Villalba, an artist I have been following recently who has done a lot of very different artwork over the past few years but whose recent paintings that are just big mish-mashes of stuff I think are incredible, and also, I think they’re portraits. Stuff and people and the context they’re in, all flattened down. Excuse the quality, as this most recent stuff is only on Instagram and not on his website.
