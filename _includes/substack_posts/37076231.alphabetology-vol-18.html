Paintings of Ham Through the Ages

I saw the famous ham paintings by Manet and Gaugin recently and thought they were funny and looked into ham paintings a bit further. Food in general was a fine art tradition for a while that seems like it has fallen off in the past century. Going back to the 17th century there are a bunch of French artists putting out stuff like this:

Which we’ve all imagined before, in our minds. I’m sure someone more knowledgable than me about fine art in the 17th century could say a lot about any symbolism and such here, but as far as paintings of ham go, these artists clearly cared much more about the drapery and the metaphors. They have no interest in depicting the simple country ham. Meanwhile in the Netherlands, Floris van Schooten is putting all of these French drapery lovers in an early grave by putting out art like this, really showing an appreciation for the food itself:

So these are the two schools of ham painting battling it out: fine drapery vs. simple drapery and emphasized foods. Jumping ahead a bit to the mid 19th century, the fine tradition of ham painting continues in France:

where the simple tablecloth of the Netherlands has been adopted, and now we have a story to go along with the ham. There’s movement, there’s action, there is mail to be read and a ham to carve up and eat. The tablecloth is clumsily folded. This delicious meal is for a person on the go. Riveting stuff. Manet sees this and a few years later puts out this masterpiece:

Mama Mia! Now we’re cooking with gas. Look at that bad boy. Manet has a bold statement for the audience: We are doing away with dinner scenes. We are all about the ham. The art world follows suit, with Gaugin and de Haan doing the unprecedented and simultaneously painting a single ham:

They even decided to throw in a diet coke. At this point I feel like there is no need to continue any further, and I believe the art world largely agreed: The finest works of ham art have been created. There is nothing left to say. There is a single addendum to this that I could find, from 1918:

Moving beyond the table and to parchment. But now we are getting too serious, too literal. There is no diet coke. And as such, I believe the world has in unison decided that rather than creating new works of ham, we shall gaze upon the Gaugin and Manet hams until the end of time.

Not that I’m complaining.

Tom DeLonge Is Going to Prove That UFOs Exist

Thank you to my friend David for suggesting this topic.

I don’t know if it’s very widely known, but Tom DeLonge of famous all-time great pop-punk band Blink 182 is very into aliens. Their song “Aliens Exist” sounds like a joke song, but you would be a fool to lump it in with their other joke songs about crude sexual acts. This one is real. And he founded a company, bringing on multiple ex-CIA employees [link] in order to do… I’m not sure what. I learned about all of this as a fan of the band, finding out that he appeared on the Joe Rogan podcast [Spotify link - youtube link is gone?]. You can listen yourself, but a long bullet point summary:

  • Tom strongly believes in aliens

  • He would research them all the time and read about them while touring with Blink

  • He has had weird run-ins with “government operatives” (there’s an anecdote about him being stopped at an airport and taken to an interrogation room by some unidentified people who kept asking him “how he knows so much about aliens” and he answers that it’s from reading books)

  • His company (To The Stars) exists to try to bridge the gap in communication that currently exists between different government agencies in terms of sharing UFO research

  • It is unclear to me how the company might actually do this, being a civilian company

  • Tom’s role in this is as media coordinator, he’s actually currently working on a movie slated for a 2021 release called “Monsters of California” about, presumably, high school, skateboarding, and aliens.

  • He talked a lot about allowing the public to invest in the company so that the government couldn’t silence them, since they would be a public company — this part seemed pretty sketchy

Actually, you can no longer invest in To The Stars. You used to be able to, on their website. Now it takes you to a page with a bunch of disclaimers about how you can’t actually give them money. I wonder what happened to all of the original investors.

In any case, I have no idea what this company does or has accomplished. Their website says their goal is to create technology using all of this alien knowledge the people involved have, but I don’t think they’ve done anything so far outside of the media space. As far as making information public, the government has been slowly releasing documents about UFOs over the past couple of years and I don’t think they have Tom’s fingerprints anywhere near them.

All that being said, it’s nice to see him pursuing his dream.

Music

* Radiohead - A Moon Shaped Pool (Rock/Ambient/Experimental)

Simply, divine. Transcendant.

This is one of the small handful of albums I ever actually bought back before Spotify was a big presence — this, Pink Floyd’s Dark Side of the Moon (the first album I ever bought), and Daft Punk’s Human After All.

* Radiohead - OK Computer (Rock/Experimental)

They are kind of ambient and cosmic but they also rock out. What’s not to love. Imagine that I am blowing a big kiss to Jonny Greenwood.

This is an anecdote about the song “Creep” from a completely different album, but the story goes that Greenwood was trying to ruin the song with the distorted guitar chugs leading into the chorus, because he hated the song. Very cool.

Radiohead - Kid A (Rock/Experimental)

I think the words “everything all of the time” kind of often, but I don’t really like this album. I can see how it was important to their future sound, which I think is very good, so it’s interesting to see them branching out and experimenting.

I think it’s funny to imagine that in “Idioteque” when Yorke sings “Ice Age coming” he is warning people about the band Iceage.

* Metallica - Ride the Lightning (Thrash)

That’s right. It’s Metallica. And they’re looking for a fight.

Metallica - Black Album (Thrash)

Okay so I don’t watch or listen to Anthony Fantano but Youtube suggested his ranking of Metallica albums and he put Black Album above Kill ‘Em All so I was like “ok I’ll give this another listen” and man. I’m going back to never listening to Anthony FRAUD-tano again.

* ZEUS! - Opera (Grindcore)

Super sick grindcore out of Italy. This is one of those aggressive guitar (bass in this case) + drum bands like Lightning Bolt or Hella, who I also really like. Great album, really goofy, really fun sounds. “Giorgio Gaslini is our Tom Araya” is such a cool track. It’s such an interesting album. I’ve come back to it every once in a while for the past however many years since first hearing it.

Psycroptic - The Scepter of the Ancients (Technical Death Metal)

It’s technical death metal (from Australia!) that my friend Alex introduced to me in High School, and it remains the only tech death that I listen to, mostly out of nostalgia. I almost exclusively listen to this at the gym. It did get me to start playing guitar again recently, I’ll give it that — I’ve never really played any metal but I’ve been having a go at the track “Lacertine Forest.”
