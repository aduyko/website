Dia:Beacon

Part two of my big upstate trip and art I liked. I’ve been to the Dia:Beacon before and think it’s a great museum with a lot of very cool art, here’s stuff I like every time:

I love love love John Chamberlain’s work. It’s very visceral and looks great and the colors are fantastic and the shapes are really good.

The bent metal sheets in this one almost look like flowers. Riveting.

Chamberlain’s use of color is really remarkable considering most of the metal he used was scrap.

I mean, come on. Beautiful beautiful work. Other works I have seen when I went years ago that I really liked this time were both of Heizer’s pieces about negative space:

Big rock!!!!

Big holes in the ground!!!! You can’t see far enough into them but they are simple 3d shapes, just as giant holes. Very cool.

Finally from the more permanent collection, Robert Ryman’s white on white paintings really grew on me this time around. I think white on white paintings are very difficult but being able to think about them a bit, you gotta hand it to the guy for really loving expressing himself almost purely through texture with no color. I’ve liked this one both times and I think it’s a little easier to get into:

The less permanent exhibition stuff that I really really liked were all of the pieces by Mary Coarse, which are impossible to show photos of. I will attempt. But they are an interactive experience, the function of the work is to reflect light differently as you move around them. Check them out if you have the chance:

I also really liked Imi Knoebel’s big painted blocks of wood:

Lots of big abstract blocks. Love it.

The pieces are really big! Very cool to see in person. I really admire Warhol’s vision and work. I think they also present a really interesting question regarding how much of the work is driven by the curator, who has to decide how to hang the individual canvases.

Finally, from the more permanent collection, I think Dorothea Rockburne’s work is hit or miss, but the hits are super compelling. She has this painting, part of her series called “Egyptian Paintings,” which were very good, and super interesting! They had slightly altered geometric shapes, like “Stele” above, where the right half of the figure kind of droops down, but the folded canvas still looks kind of aligned. Super compelling stuff. There were also some folded paper pieces that I thought were incredible but that there are not many good photos of online.

That’s it for the Dia. I also really like the Richard Serra pieces at the Dia:Beacon but did not see them this time!

Movies

———————

American Hustle

Watching this was fun but also maybe I’m just burnt out on heist movies, because I just kept thinking about Casino and Catch Me If You Can (which, by the way, is FRADULENT. Abagnale was a big time liar. Check this out: [link]). However it looks like Abscam was a real thing that the FBI did, so I think this movie did a really great job fictionalizing it. I thought it was very funny at times and the soundtrack was excellent but I kind of felt like a piece of something was missing at the end. It’s interesting in that it focuses a lot less on the heist and a lot more on the characters, but the heist is the real part and the characters are all fictionalized. So it kind of feels like it wants to lean into the characters a lot more and their internal drama but is constantly pulled back to follow the plot.

Moneyball

Not a lot to say about this one, it kind of just felt like a more boring The Big Short, but also kind of? a baseball movie? But not the most elegant combination of the two. Brad Pitt’s character seems super mad all of the time. I think it’s fine but. Just read the summary? You don’t need to see this movie. Idk. Maybe if you really love baseball.
