Figure Drawing

I have been doing figure drawing sessions on and off since around 2013. I’m not sure about an exact date and 2013 is when I started taking photos of some of my drawings. I started off doing online sessions at websites like line of action (which used to be called pixelovely) before going to my first in-person sessions in 2016, to the Bathaus sessions in Williamsburg/Bushwick. I then bounced around a few different figure drawings in Brooklyn before settling into the sessions at La Bodega Studio, which have now moved and are continuing, post-pandemic lockdowns, in Gowanus. Here are some of my drawings from then to now:

There’s a couple of years gap between these drawings in 2013 and when I started going to in-person figure drawings in 2016.

After this I think I had appendicitis and then moved in September and started going to the sessions at Trestle, back when they had sessions in Gowanus.

I picked up painting in March 2017, and was doing a little bit of that alongside figure drawing.

Between 2017 and 2019 I wasn’t doing much figure drawing, or if I was, I wasn’t photographing much of what I was doing. At the end of 2018 I quit my job and rented studio space and painted for a year, I started going to figure drawing at La Bodega around March of 2019. All of these upcoming photos are from my instagram account.

After this I got a job again and have not been making much art outside of figure drawing.

And then Coronavirus hit! And there was no figure drawing for a while, until some outdoors summer sessions, which I did not feel like I did well at:

Some online sessions, which I did not particularly enjoy

And now we’re back in person

And that’s my history with figure drawing. Hope you found this interesting. I only included long poses but you can imagine one million kinda sloppy short pose drawings as well, closer to that first starbucks drawing at the top of the post. Those look more like this now:

These are some 2 minute drawings of the June 11 model in the photo right before the previous one.

I started figure drawing as an academic thing, “I want to get better at drawing people and here’s how to do it,” and now I just do it because I think it’s fun and because I think I do good work.

Music

———————

* Lightning Bolt - Hypermagic Mountain (Noise Rock)

I really love noise bands that are just drum + guitar (bass in this case, but also thinking of like. Hella, or ZEUS!). This is an old classic that I recently revisited. Great album.

Pavement - Slanted & Enchanted (Indie Rock)

Unforunately Pavement just do not do it for my little pea-sized brain. It’s good! But nothing about it screams out to me. Although I will say I was listening to this in the car with my mom and she said she liked the lyrics “I’m trying, I’m trying, I’m trying.”

* Dinosaur Jr. - Farm (Indie Rock/Noise Rock)

I think Dinosaur Jr. are one of the greatest bands of all time!!! I actually want to write something longer about them, I’m just not sure what I would say. This album is great, I think it’s incredible that they put out music like this after a really long hiatus, I think the way “Pieces” ties into “Said The People” is unbelievable and worth listening to the album for alone. This band rocks, straight and simple.

King Harvest - Dancing in the Moonlight

The title track is fully sick, an all-timer of a song. Song of the summer 2021. The rest didn’t really stick with me.
