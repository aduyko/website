Books

— — — — — —

The Sympathizer* - Viet Thanh Nguyen

I liked this. It felt very bleak and it made war and politics feel awful, as they should.

To Kill a Mockingbird* - Harper Lee

This read a lot more like Tom Sawyer or The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn than I expected it to? There’s a lot of just being a kid and hanging out and growing up and seeing how people change and how the world changes, and noticing yourself change along with everything else. This was a really really fun read and also very good. Great.

Bad Blood* - John Carreyrou

I knew Theranos was an absolute nightmare, and I’d read a bunch of stuff about how their promises were crazy, and about how Elizabeth Holmes touted that she drew a diagram of a time machine when she was 5 as some sign of her inventorial might. This is my favorite part of the entire Theranos scandal, that I think about sometimes:

I did NOT know how much of a nightmare it was on the inside. Or about the honestly baffling persuasive prowess of Holmes. This was a fun read, and I’m sure it’s insanely biased, but the company was horrible and I’m not sure how much value there is in trying to redeem them with more sympathetic perspectives. They defrauded innocent people regarding their health.

Range: Why Generalists Triumph In a Specialized World - David Epstein

This book is a stinker!!!!!!!!!! It’s pop psych. Prepare to read dozens of anecdotes about the most impressive people in the universe, and how you should be like them. Prepare to learn that virtuoso musicians have the kind of mental flexibility that it takes to switch from acoustic guitar to electric guitar.

I think there are a lot of genuinely interesting ideas that Epstein presents, and there are things he brings up that I would like to incorporate more into my life, but as a package this book sucks. Its 200 pages or whatever repeating “It’s good to try doing a lot of different stuff,” with a smattering of interesting anecdotes and then the most inane thing you have read in your life to back those ideas up.

It is absolutely insane to me that art is used as a category of thing that Epstein considers a good example of how a wide range of experiences can contribute. I don’t even want to write more about it.

The Melancholy of Resistance* - László Krasznahorkai

We start off with a rowdy bunch of Hungarian peasants whomst smell like sausage and are farting and burping on a train car and the immense terror this causes poor Ms. Plauf, we follow pages and pages of neverending sentences of Mr Eszter thinking about how the light reflects off of all of the garbage in the streets, we learn about the cosmos, we learn about authoritarian might. Paranoia ebbs and flows as nothing good happens, first in theory, later very agressively in reality. What more do you want.
